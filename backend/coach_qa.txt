Q: What is the STAR method for answering behavioral questions?
A: STAR stands for Situation, Task, Action, Result. You set the context (Situation), explain what you needed to achieve (Task), describe what you specifically did (Action), and finish with the impact or outcome (Result).

Q: How should I answer 'Tell me about yourself'?
A: Use Past → Present → Future. Summarize your background, explain your current work, and end with what role you want next and why.

Q: What makes a strong resume bullet point?
A: Start with an action verb, describe what you did, and quantify the impact.

Q: How do I explain a technical project simply?
A: Focus on problem → solution → impact. Use analogies and avoid jargon.

Q: What strengths do interviewers appreciate?
A: Ownership, communication, problem solving, adaptability, fast learning—supported by examples.

Q: How do I talk about weaknesses?
A: Choose a real weakness, explain how you identified it, and show the steps you’re taking to improve.

Q: How long should a STAR answer be?
A: 1–2 minutes: enough detail to show thinking and impact without overwhelming.
